Dr. Vikram Ambalal Sarabhai

Father of India's Space Program


Indian physicist, astronomer industrialist and innovator.

Here's a time line of Dr. Sarabhai's life:



  • 1919 - Born on 12th August 1919 in Ahmedabad in British India

  • He was born in a family of famous Industrialist of that time.

  • Vikram pursued his Doctorate from Cambridge University in England.

  • Vikram Sarabhai studied Natural Science from Cambridge University in England.

  • He had also been the Doctoral advisor of Great Indian Physicist, Sir C.V. Raman.

  • Apart from science, Vikram also had an interest in the industry, business, and development issues.

  • The Indian Institute of Management (IIM) at Ahmadabad is a foundation of Vikram Sarabhai.

  • The project of launching the first Indian Satellite ‘Aryabhata’ was under Vikram Sarabhai.

  • He was guided by father of Nuclear Science in India Dr. Homi Jahangir Bhabha while setting up his first Rocket Launching Station in India.

  • The great Dr. Vikram Ambalal Sarabhai is the founder of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

  • 1966 - Vikram Sarabhai received Padma Bhushan.

  • 1947 - Vikram Sarabhai also founded Physical Research Laboratory.

  • He was also appointed chairman of Atomic Energy Commission of India after the death of Dr. Homi Jahangir Bhabha to carry forward his projects.

  • 1971 - He died on 30th December in Kerala.

  • 1972 - He was a receiver of prestigious Padma Vibhushan in 1972 posthumously.

  • Vikram Sarabhai was the chairman of Space Application Centre that he had founded himself.

  • The famous ‘Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre’ in Kerala is named after him and in his memory.

  • The Lander of ‘Chandrayan 2’ is named Vikram to pay him honour.

  • On the first death anniversary of Vikram Sarabhai, the Indian Postal Department issued a stamp commemorating him.
